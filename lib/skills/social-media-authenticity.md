This skill prevents AI-generated social media content from sounding like AI-generated social media content. Every pattern below is statistically overused by language models to the point of being a fingerprint. Avoid all of them.

---

## THE PRIME DIRECTIVE

Before writing a single word: identify what the actual point is, and open with it. Not a setup. Not a hook. Not a dramatic pause. The point.

If you cannot state the core idea in one plain sentence before writing, you don't understand it well enough to post about it yet.

---

## BANNED OPENERS

Never start a post with these. They are delay tactics that signal nothing interesting follows.

The Throat-Clearers — phrases that exist only to buy time before saying something:

- "Here's the thing:"
- "Here's what most people miss:"
- "Here's what nobody's talking about:"
- "Here's the uncomfortable truth:"
- "Let me be clear:"
- "I'll be honest:"
- "Can we talk about X for a second?"
- "Let's talk about X."
- "We need to talk about X."

The False Exclusivity Hooks — pretending the reader is receiving classified intelligence:

- "This is the part most people skip."
- "Most people won't tell you this."
- "Nobody's talking about this."
- "Everyone's sleeping on this."
- "What they don't want you to know:"
- "I wasn't supposed to share this, but..."
- "The secret that [industry] doesn't want you to know:"

The Manufactured Urgency Hooks — artificial time pressure on information with no expiry:

- "Stop what you're doing."
- "Drop everything."
- "Read this before [X]."
- "You're going to want to bookmark this."
- "Save this before it gets taken down."
- "This changes everything."
- "[X] just changed the game forever."

---

## BANNED BODY PATTERNS

The Binary Contrast — the single most overused AI writing structure. Avoid all variations:

- "It's not about X. It's about Y."
- "The problem isn't X. It's Y."
- "Stop thinking of it as X. Start thinking of it as Y."
- "Most people think it's X. It's actually Y."
- "X is dead. Y is what's next."
- "Forget X. Focus on Y."

> Why it's bad: Contrast creates the _illusion_ of insight without requiring any. It's a syntactic trick, not a thought.

The Dramatic Fragmentation — choppy sentences for artificial weight:

- "Let that sink in."
- "Read that again."
- "Full stop."
- "Period."
- "Sit with that for a second."
- "I'll say it louder for the people in the back."

> Why it's bad: It signals the writer couldn't build a compelling paragraph, so they broke it into pieces and called it style.

The Fake Vulnerability — manufactured relatability:

- "I'm going to be honest with you."
- "I wasn't going to post this, but..."
- "This is scary to share."
- "Unpopular opinion:" (followed by something 95% of people agree with)
- "I know I'll get hate for this, but..."
- "Hot take incoming (don't hate me):"

The Ladder of Escalation — three things, ascending intensity, meaning nothing:

- "It's fast. It's free. It's open source."
- "No signup. No API key. No credit card."
- "Not a prototype. Not a demo. Production-ready."

The Numbered List Inflation — "10 things" lists with 2 actual insights and 8 filler items. If using a list, every item must earn its spot. Cut any item that is obvious, redundant, or padding.

---

## BANNED TRANSITIONS

These are used when there is no actual connective logic between paragraphs. Don't use them — find the real transition or restructure.

- "But here's where it gets interesting:"
- "And here's the kicker:"
- "But that's not even the best part."
- "Wait, it gets better."
- "But the real story is:"
- "Enter: [X]."
- "The plot thickens."

---

## BANNED CLOSERS

The CTA Slop — engagement-farming sign-offs:

- "What do you think? Drop your take below 👇"
- "Agree or disagree? Let me know."
- "Follow for more [X] content."
- "Repost if this resonated ♻️"
- "Tag someone who needs to hear this."
- "Save this for later 🔖"

The Fake Philosophical Closer — sounds profound, says nothing:

- "The question isn't whether, but when."
- "We're still early."
- "The best time to start was yesterday. The second best time is now."
- "The genie is out of the bottle."
- "Welcome to the future."
- "And we're just getting started."

The Performative Mic Drop:

- "That's not a prediction. That's a fact."
- "And that changes everything."
- "Think about that."
- "Act accordingly."
- "Plan accordingly."
- "[X] will never be the same."

---

## BANNED WORDS

Adverbs that fingerprint AI writing — if three or more appear in a paragraph, rewrite it:
Literally, Incredibly, Fundamentally, Genuinely, Essentially, Significantly, Arguably, Undeniably, Remarkably, Interestingly, Notably, Particularly, Ultimately

Adjectives that mean nothing — vague intensifiers that could describe anything:
Robust, Seamless, Cutting-edge, Groundbreaking, Revolutionary, Transformative, Comprehensive, Holistic, Game-changing, Next-level, World-class

Verbs that sound smart but aren't:
Leverage, Navigate (challenges), Unpack (concepts), Double down, Spearhead, Supercharge, Unlock, Streamline, Reimagine, Synergize

---

## BANNED SENTENCE TEMPLATES

These are fill-in-the-blank structures that produce the same post regardless of topic. Never use them:

1. "[X] isn't just [obvious thing]. It's [grander reframe]."
2. "The best [role] don't [common action]. They [elevated action]."
3. "In [year], [X] won't be optional. It'll be table stakes."
4. "I stopped [common approach] and started [better approach]. The results speak for themselves."
5. "[X] is the new [Y]."
6. "If you're still [old method], you're already behind."
7. "[X] did in [short time] what used to take [long time]."
8. "The [role] of [future year] will look nothing like the [role] of [past year]."
9. "[X] that [verb] will thrive. [X] that don't will be left behind."
10. "Your [X] is only as good as your [Y]."

## EMOJI RULES

Use sparingly and intentionally. The following are overused to the point of being invisible:

- 🔥 = everything is fire, nothing is fire
- 🚀 = every launch, every growth post
- 💡 = every "insight"
- 🧵 = fine for threads, but not as decoration
- ✅ bullets on every list item

Rule: One emoji per post maximum unless the format specifically calls for more. If the emoji is replacing punctuation or emphasis rather than adding meaning, cut it.

## WHAT GOOD LOOKS LIKE

Replace AI patterns with these principles:

| Instead of...                      | Do this...                                                     |
| ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Dramatic opener to delay the point | Open with the point itself                                     |
| "It's not X, it's Y" contrast      | State what it _is_, with a specific example                    |
| "Nobody's talking about this"      | Just say the thing — if it's interesting, it speaks for itself |
| Numbered list padding              | Write fewer items with more depth                              |
| Philosophical non-closer           | End when the thought is done. No mic drop required.            |
| Vague power adjectives             | Specific, concrete details                                     |
| "The results speak for themselves" | State the actual result                                        |
| Fake vulnerability                 | Real specificity (dates, amounts, what went wrong)             |

## SELF-AUDIT BEFORE PUBLISHING

Run this check on every output:

1. Does the opening sentence contain the actual point? If not, delete everything before the first sentence that does.
2. Count banned words. More than 3 in one post = rewrite.
3. Is the closer adding something, or just performing? If performing, cut it.
4. Is any "insight" just a contrast structure in disguise? If yes, find the actual observation underneath it.
5. Would this post sound identical if you swapped the topic? If yes, it's a template, not a post.
